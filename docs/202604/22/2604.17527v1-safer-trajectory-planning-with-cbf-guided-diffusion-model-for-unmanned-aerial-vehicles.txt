# Safer Trajectory Planning with CBF-guided Diffusion Model for Unmanned Aerial Vehicles 

Peiwen Yang, Shiyu Bai, Member, IEEE, Weisong Wen, Member, IEEE, Yixin Gao, and Jiahao Hu 

Abstract —Safe and agile trajectory planning is essential for autonomous systems, especially during complex aerobatic maneu-vers. Motivated by the recent success of diffusion models in gen-erative tasks, this paper introduces AeroTrajGen, a novel frame-work for diffusion-based trajectory generation that incorporates control barrier function (CBF)-guided sampling during inference, specifically designed for unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). The proposed CBF-guided sampling addresses two critical challenges: (1) mitigating the inherent unpredictability and potential safety violations of diffusion models, and (2) reducing reliance on exten-sively safety-verified training data. During the reverse diffusion process, CBF-based guidance ensures collision-free trajectories by seamlessly integrating safety constraint gradients with the diffusion model’s score function. The model features an obstacle-aware diffusion transformer architecture with multi-modal con-ditioning, including trajectory history, obstacles, maneuver styles, and goal, enabling the generation of smooth, highly agile trajecto-ries across 14 distinct aerobatic maneuvers. Trained on a dataset of 2,000 expert demonstrations, AeroTrajGen is rigorously evalu-ated in simulation under multi-obstacle environments. Simulation results demonstrate that CBF-guided sampling reduces collision rates by 94.7% compared to unguided diffusion baselines, while preserving trajectory agility and diversity. Our code is open-sourced at https://github.com/RoboticsPolyu/CBF-DMP. 

Index Terms —Control barrier functions (CBFs), diffusion model, unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), trajectory planning, safe inference. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

# GENERATIVE artificial intelligence (GenAI) has demon-strated remarkable capabilities in producing high-quality, diverse content across various domains, including text, im-ages, and audio [1], [2]. Despite these successes, current AI approaches remain limited in handling long-tail and safety-critical domains. Consequently, the unpredictability of gener-ative models may lead to outputs that violate safety constraints, creating a fundamental barrier to their deployment in safety-critical tasks, such as trajectory planning. Diffusion models [2] have recently gained significant atten-tion in generative modeling due to their ability to produce high-quality and diverse samples. Diffusion models can be particularly effective for trajectory generation, as they can capture complex, multimodal distributions of trajectories and generate dynamically smooth maneuvers through a unified denoising process. However, diffusion models can produce unsafe outputs if not properly constrained, such as trajectories that collide with obstacles. Control barrier functions (CBFs)

[3], [4] have emerged as powerful tools for enforcing safety constraints in planning and control systems. CBFs provide a systematic way to define safe sets and ensure that the system’s state remains within these sets through appropriate control inputs. By integrating CBFs into the generative process, we can guide the model to produce outputs that are not only diverse and high-quality but also safe and reliable. In this work, we introduce AeroTrajGen , a diffusion-based trajectory generation model with safe inference capabilities. The proposed approach integrates CBFs into the diffusion process, allowing the model to generate trajectories that com-ply with safety constraints. This safety enforcement during inference serves two key purposes: (1) mitigating the inherent unsafety of AI algorithm outputs, and (2) reducing dependency on extensively safety-verified training data. It addresses the challenges through two key innovations:  

> •

An obstacle-aware diffusion transformer is introduced that jointly encodes environmental context into the gen-erative prior, enabling efficient synthesis of physics-informed, multi-style trajectories without collision-free demonstrations being required.  

> •

Rather than applying safety as a post-hoc filter, we embed the forward invariance of CBFs directly into the gradient flow of the reverse-time SDE: the safety probability function is incorporated into the generative process. Retraining with safety-verified data is no longer necessary. 

A. Related Works 1) Diffusion Models for Trajectory Generation: Diffusion models have recently been applied to trajectory optimization for mobile manipulation [5] and aerobatic flight generation [6] for UAVs, demonstrating their ability to produce diverse and high-quality paths. The work [6] proposed a duffusion-based approach for generating aerobatic flight trajectories. Some studies have explored post-processing techniques to enhance safety in diffusion-generated trajectories. The work [5] em-ployed task-oriented objectives and physics-based constraints to refine trajectories. Similarly, [7] formulated motion plan-ning as planning-as-inference by sampling from a posterior distribution leveraging guidance in diffusion models. Diffusion model adapts to high-dimensional, multimodal trajectory dis-tributions, circumventing the curse of dimensionality and post-hoc smoothing requirements that fundamentally limit RRT and A* in agile aerial robotics [8]. Techniques such as “projection curriculum” [9] and clas-sifier guidance [10] have shown promise in steering model

Through the “projection cur-riculum,” the difference between projection trajectory through safety constraints and original noise-free trajectory is used as a loss to update the neural network. Classifier guidance relies on an additional pre-trained model to provide gradients that guide the diffusion process away from unsafe areas. However, these methods often rely on heuristic approaches and may not provide formal safety guarantees. 

2) Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) in Trajectory Plan-ning: CBFs [3], [4] have been widely used in model predictive control (MPC) to ensure safety in control systems. Researchers have applied convex model predictive CBF approaches to differentially flat systems [11], ensuring safety while opti-mizing performance. A factor graph-based CBF formulation was proposed for safe trajectory optimization of UAVs [14], enabling efficient handling of complex con-straints. Time-varying CBF was employed to guarantee safe and precise landing of UAVs on moving targets [15]. CBFs have proven highly effective in enforcing safety constraints, and their ability to formally guarantee safety has recently inspired integration into deep learning frameworks [16]. Our work explores integrating CBFs into diffusion models to guide trajectory generation towards safe regions during inference. 

B. Overview 

Figure 1 illustrates the overall framework of the proposed diffusion-based aerobatic trajectory planning framework with CBF-guided inference for UAVs. The framework consists of two main components: the training phase and the inference phase. During training, the diffusion model learns from a dataset of aerobatic trajectories (2,000 samples covering 14 maneuver styles), incorporating an obstacle-aware diffusion transformer conditioned on historical trajectory, target way-point, maneuver styles, and obstacle information. During in-ference, the trained model generates trajectories with CBF-guided sampling that modifies the reverse diffusion process by integrating safety constraints directly into generation steps, steering trajectories away from obstacles. B. Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) 

CBFs [3], [4] provide a systematic framework for enforcing safety constraints in dynamical systems. A CBF is a continu-ously differentiable function h : Rn → R that defines a safe set C as its superlevel set: 

C = {x ∈ Rn : h(x) ≥ 0}, (10) with ∂C = {x : h(x) = 0 } denoting its boundary and Int (C) = 

{x : h(x) > 0} its interior. The goal of CBF-based control is to render C forward invariant, i.e., if x(0) ∈ C , then x(t) ∈ C 

for all t ≥ 0.For discrete-time systems, we consider the dynamics:

D. Diffusion-based Aerobatic Trajectory Generation 

AeroTrajGen is a diffusion model framework for generat-ing safe and complex aerobatic trajectories in obstacle-laden environments. 

1) Model Architecture: The core is an Obstacle-Aware Diffusion Transformer that conditions trajectory generation on multiple contextual inputs while maintaining temporal coherence. The model operates on state sequences x ∈ RN ×D ,where N = 80 (8 seconds at 10 Hz) and D = 10 (speed, 3D position, 3D attitude). 

2) Conditional Embedding with Obstacle Encoding: We design a multi-modal conditioning module fusing four critical information sources:  

> •

Diffusion timestep t: Embedded via multi-layer percep-tron (MLP) to guide the denoising process  

> •

Target waypoint pt ∈ R3: The desired trajectory end-point  

> •

Maneuver style a ∈ R14 : One-hot encoded action specifying maneuver style  

> •

Obstacle context : Encoded via an attention-based obsta-cle encoder 

3) Transformer Backbone with Causal Masking: The de-noising network employs a transformer decoder with causal masking. Input noisy trajectory xt is projected to latent dimension and combined with positional encoding: 

h = TransformerDecoder (xt + econd , M) (33) where econd is the expanded condition and M is a causal mask. The history trajectory is concatenated with the noisy input, jointly encoded by a Transformer with causal attention and continuous positional encoding, and the output corresponding to the future segment is supervised with a continuity loss that penalizes jumps between the history’s last state and the prediction’s first state.

D ISCUSSION 

A. Key Findings 

Our experimental results demonstrate several key findings: 

Zero-Collision Safety : With the logistic safety probability function and γmax = 4000 , CBF-guided sampling achieves 0% collision rate in simulation (tested on 200 trajectories across 14 maneuvers), representing a 100% reduction from the unguided baseline (96.0% collision rate). This validates the effectiveness of incorporating safety constraints into the diffusion sampling process. 

Safety-Accuracy Trade-off : Stronger guidance ( γ = 4000 )eliminates collisions but increases position RMSE from 0.47 m to 1.19 m (+153%) and reduces overall success rate to 42.6%. The optimal configuration depends on application requirements: safety-critical scenarios favor γ = 4000 , while trajectory-fidelity-critical scenarios benefit from the obstacle encoder with moderate guidance ( γ = 2000 ). 

Maneuver Diversity : The model successfully generates all 14 aerobatic maneuvers with zero collisions. More complex maneuvers (sphinx, eight figure) show lower success rates (30-32%) due to higher precision requirements conflicting with safety constraints. 

B. Limitations 

Current limitations of our approach include the assumption of static spherical obstacles with known positions and radii, as dynamic obstacles and partially observable environments remain future work; while the CBF formulation uses spherical obstacles for computational efficiency, generalizing to arbitrary convex or non-convex shapes is an important direction. This article presents AeroTrajGen, a novel diffusion-based framework for generating safe and complex aerobatic trajec-tories in obstacle-laden environments. By integrating CBFs into the reverse diffusion process, our approach effectively steers trajectory generation away from unsafe regions while maintaining the diversity and quality of generated maneuvers. The Obstacle-Aware Diffusion Transformer architecture en-hances the model’s ability to incorporate environmental con-text, enabling generation of dynamically feasible and collision-free aerobatic trajectories across 14 distinct maneuver styles. Quantitative evaluation demonstrates that CBF-guided sam-pling reduces collision rates by 94.7% compared to unguided diffusion baselines while preserving trajectory agility (92.4% average success rate). The proposed framework bridges the gap between expressive generative models and safety-critical robotics, offering a unified solution for safe aerobatic trajec-tory generation.
